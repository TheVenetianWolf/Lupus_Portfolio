Origin Story

file: about.md

I didn't fall into technology. I walked towards it, deliberately, curiously, and with the kind of stubbornness that refuses to leave a problem unsolved. 💻

I'm a Computer Science student at Trinity College Dublin, currently researching autonomous networks at the Huawei Ireland Research Centre, work that sits at the frontier of how the world stays connected. But the title only tells part of the story. The rest lives in the hours outside the office and the lecture hall.

I'm training as a security researcher 🔐, hunting vulnerabilities across real-world applications through bug bounty programmes on HackerOne and Bugcrowd, testing web application flaws, misconfigurations and logic issues, then writing them up clearly and disclosing them responsibly. Alongside that, I train in Capture The Flag environments on Hack The Box and TryHackMe, sharpening the instincts that only come from actually breaking things in a controlled setting.

"The best problem-solvers aren't defined by their job title. They're defined by the hunt. By the refusal to walk past a broken thing without wanting to fix it."

I founded the Wild Hunt Project 🐺, a community built around that exact idea. What I'm drawn to doesn't fit neatly into one box. Criminology and forensic science sit beside machine learning and systems architecture in how I think. Military history informs how I approach strategy. Literature shapes how I communicate. I believe the most unexpected ideas come from people who refuse to stay in their lane, and I've never been particularly good at staying in mine.

I'm not the loudest person in the room. But I'm usually the one who's already thought three moves ahead, or who asks the question nobody else thought to ask. I work hard in the background, building, testing, failing, iterating, because that's where the real work actually happens.

If you're working on something at the edge of what's possible in security, technology or innovation, or if you just want to talk to someone who gets genuinely excited about where all of this is going, I'd love to connect. The future doesn't build itself. But it's a good time to try. 👋😎
